"This may be just data to you, but for us, it is our kundli. You have shown us where we stand and how we need to build our resilience."

Mrs. Janaki  ·  Woman fisherfolk, Odisha  ·  On seeing community resilience data for the first time

I started measuring maternal and child malnutrition in villages in Rajasthan. Now I am mapping how climate-related shocks impact food systems — what farmers lose before their crops ever reach a market, and why that gap keeps widening.

The work has changed. The question has not: where does evidence actually reach the people who need it?

I am currently a Data Fellow at J-PAL South Asia (MIT), placed with the Rural Futures team at WELL Labs in Bengaluru. I produce policy data stories, build financial models for smallholder interventions, and create spatial analyses for rural livelihoods work in Karnataka. I have also been writing — essays, op-eds, data stories — for longer than I have been doing research.

Public Health Trainee · NITI Aayog Aspirational District, Karauli, Rajasthan

Where it started. Conducting maternal and child health training across 40 villages in one of India's most underserved districts. Sitting on floors with women from Self-Help Groups, measuring adolescent girls' heights, teaching WASH and nutrition basics to communities with little prior access.
